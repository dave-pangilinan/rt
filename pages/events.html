Biniray Festival

Feast of Sr. Sto. Niño de Romblon
Date: January 12-22, 2018
Location: Municipality of Romblon

Ton-Ton

The 11 day Feast of Señor Sto. Niño de Romblon is started by the tradition called “Ton-Ton”. Ton-Ton, held on January 12, is a ceremony of taking the image of Señor Sto. Niño from his altar to the main altar of Catedral San Jose. This ceremony is the start of the Biniray Festival of the town of Romblon.

Biniray

Biniray, held on January 13, is the traditional fluvial procession highlighted by a flotilla of vessels that circle Romblon Bay seven times, marking the Spanish galleon’s fruitless attempts to remove Señor Sto. Niño from the province. This is followed by a colorful parade of marchers adorned with flowers and brightly painted costumes and faces.

Marble Festival

The 8th Marble Festival, which started on January 12 in part with the Biniray Festival, showcases the artistic works of our local marble artisans. Comparable to Italy's Carrara, Romblon's famous marble comes in a spectrum of shades ranging from white to black, with a gamut of in-between tints like mottled white, tiger white and green onyx.

Kanidugan Festival

Kanidugan Festival is being celebrated annually by Odionganon in honor of the town’s saint, Saint Vincent Ferrer. The festival’s name derived from the word "Nidog" or Niyog which means coconut. Coconut trees are abundant in Tablas Island, and copra is one of the major products and main source of livelihood of the people.
